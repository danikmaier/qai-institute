mission_en: |
  The QAI Institute is a trilingual digital archive dedicated to documenting, preserving, and critically engaging with the architectural heritage of Kazakhstan and Central Asia. We believe that architecture is the most tangible expression of a culture's identity — its materials, forms, and spatial logic carry the accumulated intelligence of generations.

  Our archive spans the full range of Kazakh built heritage: from the portable spatial genius of the nomadic yurt to the Silk Road mausoleums, from the complex negotiations of Soviet national modernism to the starchitect spectacles of the new capital Astana. We make this heritage accessible in Kazakh, Russian, and English — because architecture belongs to everyone who inhabits it.

  We exist to support an architecture that is neither nostalgic nor globally generic: one that is specifically, and proudly, of this place.

  Институт QAI — трёхъязычный цифровой архив, посвящённый документированию, сохранению и критическому осмыслению архитектурного наследия Казахстана и Центральной Азии. Мы убеждены, что архитектура является наиболее ощутимым выражением культурной идентичности.

bio_en: |
  The QAI Institute was founded in 2023 by a group of architects, architectural historians, and cultural researchers based between Almaty, Astana, and London. We operate as an independent research organisation with no government affiliation.

  Our editorial board includes practising architects, academics from Al-Farabi Kazakh National University and the Kazakh Leading Academy of Architecture, and representatives of heritage civil society organisations in Kazakhstan.

  We accept submissions for the Research and Future sections from qualified researchers and practitioners. Please contact us at the address below.
