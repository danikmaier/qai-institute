description_en: |
  The Central State Museum of Kazakhstan stands as one of Almaty's most significant cultural landmarks. Designed in the 1980s, the building synthesises Soviet modernist construction principles with visual references to traditional Kazakh architectural and ornamental heritage.

  The circular plan echoes the form of the traditional yurt — a conscious design decision that grounds the museum physically and symbolically in nomadic culture. The exterior bas-reliefs and ceramic tile work incorporate geometric patterns derived from Kazakh textile traditions.

  The museum houses over 300,000 artefacts spanning prehistoric periods through the modern Kazakh state, making it the largest repository of Kazakh cultural heritage in the country.
