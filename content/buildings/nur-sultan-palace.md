description_en: |
  The Palace of Peace and Reconciliation in Astana (formerly Nur-Sultan) is a 77-metre high glass pyramid designed by Sir Norman Foster of Foster + Partners. Commissioned by President Nursultan Nazarbayev for the Congress of World and Traditional Religions, the building represents Kazakhstan's ambition to position itself as a crossroads of civilisations.

  The pyramid's glass apex houses a sun-lit atrium capable of seating 200 delegates. The structure contains a 1,500-seat opera house, a national museum of culture and science, and a presidential apartment. The stained glass panels on the apex, designed in collaboration with artist Brian Clarke, depict a peace-oriented iconographic programme drawing from world religious traditions.

  Foster's approach deliberately avoids local vernacular references in favour of a universalist formal language — a contrast with the museum and theatre buildings of Almaty that actively engaged with Kazakh ornamental traditions.
